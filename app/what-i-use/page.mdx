# What I use

A big chunk of being a developer is figuring out what tools to use. Yes, knowledge and being a team player are extremely important, but so is knowing what tools to use. 

Of course I love Figma, GitHub, ChatGPT, Google Calendar... but here I'll focus on the tools that are lesser-known, but make my life easier.

I'll bet you a coffee that if you give these tools an honest try, you'll fall in love just like I did. If they don't convince you, I'll owe you a coffee.


- [Arc Browser](https://arc.net/): Every person I have shown this browser to absolutely loves it. It's fast, it's beautiful, it just works. Built on Chromium, but re-skinned with a focus on usefulness.
- [Raycast](https://www.raycast.com/): Replaces Spotlight on Mac. It's faster, more powerful, and just works. Best part: you can install or create extensions. My favourites are *Show My IP*, *Word Counter*, *Kill Process*, *Lorem Ipsum generator*, *Clipboard History*, *Color Picker* and *Just Play...* (to play music). The team behind Raycast is out of this world; some of the best developers and UX designers ever are working at Raycast, including <Avatar person={Person.PedroDuarte} />.
- [Twitter](https://twitter.com/): (Less well-known as 𝕏). If you train your feed to only show you the best content, it's a great tool. But you have to ensure that you're only seeing the best content. Don't be afraid of smashing the "Not interested" button whenever the feed gets weird.
- [Apple Mac M<Latex children={"$x$"} /> Series](https://www.apple.com/mac/): Yes, I know they're more expensive than Windows alternatives. But if you spend most of your day in front of a computer anyway, you might as well spend a bit more to get an improved experience. I am proud to have converted a growing list of people from PC to M<Latex children={"$x$"} /> Mac: <Avatar person={Person.KhalidBelhadj} />, <Avatar person={Person.RobertaPosiunaite} />, <Avatar person={Person.DornaHamedBarghi} /> and <Avatar person={Person.WietskeHolwerda} hideSurname />.
- [Cursor](https://www.cursor.com/): VS Code fork that integrates AI into every part of your workflow. The context window for code generation is not only based on the current file, but also on the entire project. Super useful for quickly adding features to projects. Made by a very [talented group of friends](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oFfVt3S51T4).
- [Handy](https://handy.computer/): Speech-to-text for when typing is too slow. It is great for writing long prompts by talking through your full train of thought out loud. [Superwhisper](https://superwhisper.com/) feels more polished and has lovely little UI sounds, but Handy being open source and local-first is brilliant: no subscription, no cloud dependency. You can also have LLM post-processing with your own API keys.
- [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/): A powerful note-taking app that I use to draft my blog posts. Files that you create are saved to your computer as `.md` files, so no more vendor lock-in. Supports all kinds of markdown (including <Latex children={"$\\LaTeX$"} />) and has an amazing plugin ecosystem. I've used it to write Uni notes and make flashcards. Pro tip: if you are using >iOS 18 and iCloud sync for Obsidian, I highly recommend enabling the `Keep Downloaded` feature on the Obsidian folder.
- [UptimeRobot](https://uptimerobot.com/): Monitors the uptime of your endpoints. The free plan is extremely generous and checks endpoints every 5 minutes!
- [Web Interface Guidelines](https://interfaces.rauno.me/) by <Avatar person={Person.RaunoFreiberg} />: Useful checklist when creating interfaces. Reminds you how to build smooth, accessible and elegant interfaces.
- [Unavatar](https://unavatar.io/): Smoothly retrieve avatar pictures of people. Given a username on a platform, the service provides their avatar picture. If a picture is updated, so will the avatar served by Unavatar.
- [Retro](https://retro.app/): A way to share your week's pictures with friends. This is the first social media network that actually makes me feel closer to my friends. The team is also looking to monetise in a way that doesn't involve ads, but things like printed cards. Very exciting.
- [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/): It might seem like an obvious choice, but following the right channels makes all the difference. 
  - <Avatar person={Person.Fireship} /> is perfect for quick, witty overviews of tech topics and emerging trends.
  - If you prefer a _more_ opinionated and deep-dive approach, <Avatar person={Person.Theo} /> does no-nonsense takes on full-stack (with emphasis on frontend) development, discussing the pros and cons of tools like Next.js and tRPC based on real-world experience. Some of his takes are more controversial than others, but his opinions are usually valuable and on-point.
  - For a more backend-focused view, <Avatar person={Person.HusseinNasser} /> covers software architecture, databases, and system design. My personal favourites are when he opens the network tab and runs through every single request and response made on big websites like Netflix. Loads to learn!
- <Avatar person={Person.LexFridman} />'s podcast has long-form conversations with a (very) diverse range of experts. Lex dives deep into complex topics like technology or the future of humanity and somehow manages to always ask the right questions and follow along regardless of the complexity of the topic. My personal favourites are the ones with tech CEOs and AI researchers.
- [Bezel](https://nonstrict.eu/bezel/): Easiest way to get screenshots (and recently, videos) from Apple devices with their device frames. Beautifully simple and effective. This is what I&apos;m using to take screenshots of my projects.
- [Figma Slides](https://www.figma.com/slides/): Figma&apos;s presentation tool. Light years away from PowerPoint and more customisable than Google Slides. What it lacks in features (animations or videos), it makes up for in the freedom to design the slides as you want.
- [GrandPerspective](https://grandperspectiv.sourceforge.io/): A tool to visualise disk usage. Free, or available through the App Store, it will actually help you free up space on your Mac.
- [OrbStack](https://orbstack.dev/): A faster and more lightweight alternative to Docker Desktop for Mac. I personally had <Avatar person={Person.StanFlint} /> convert me to it, but if not, [their own comparison table](https://docs.orbstack.dev/compare/docker-desktop) should do the trick for you.

Lastly, friends and family.
